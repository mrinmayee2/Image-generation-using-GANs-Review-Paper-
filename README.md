📌 About the Project

This repository contains a comprehensive review paper on Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), focusing on their role in image generation.

GANs, introduced in 2014, use two neural networks — a Generator and a Discriminator — that compete with each other to generate realistic data. This paper studies their evolution, architectures, and real-world applications.

⚠️ Challenges
Mode collapse (limited output diversity)
Training instability
High computational cost
Evaluation difficulties
Ethical concerns
📊 Key Insights
GANs significantly improve image realism
Architectures like StyleGAN and IGAN achieve state-of-the-art results
GANs play a major role in medical imaging applications
New approaches like diffusion models are emerging as alternatives

This review highlights the rapid advancements in GANs and their growing impact across industries. Despite challenges, continuous research is making GANs more stable, efficient, and scalable.
